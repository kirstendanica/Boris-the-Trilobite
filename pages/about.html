Why trilobites? An entire website dedicated to them?

Trilobites are, simply put, one of the most successful creatures in the history of Earth. If you gain an interest in paleontology or geology beyond the dinosaurs (though they're amazing too!) you will most likely spot a trilobite fossil quite quickly. They are oftentimes overlooked by the general public as they don't necessarily fit the image, on first glance at least, as an incredible prehistoric creature. And, to be quite frank?

Well, they're... bugs.

Sure, you can hear people discussing how big and intimidating bugs of the past were when speaking about giant cockroaches or massive centipedes, but in my experience, that is usually the extent of it. Nobody really considers the 'granddaddy bug', the OG, the bug that came before the bugs we know today.

Enter: the Trilobite
One of the earliest known groups of arthropods (bugs!) evolved at a rapid speed, distributing themselves all across the world in great variety and abundance.

I'd always dreamed of a world where a concept like the Pokemon game was real. As a child, I practically lived by the motto "Gotta catch 'em all!" It got to the point where I can clearly remember writing to scientists before the age of 11 or so, via email, asking them to "make Pokemon real." What can I say? I loved the idea of a certain type of creature that came in all kinds of shapes and sizes, behaviors, color patterns, found in different areas of the world ... Enter: the trilobite. As Boris states in the homepage, with over 20,000 species, it truly would be a life goal to "catch 'em all," in their current fossilized form, of course...

The purpose of this website is to entertain while educating. All artwork is done by myself and the entire website has been created by me as well. This website is, firstly, a personal project created for my own enjoyment and web development skill growth. Whoever happens to enjoy this website, laugh a bit, learn a little bit of something new along the way - that is the ultimate purpose.
